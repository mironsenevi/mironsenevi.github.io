Super Heavy booster catch

On October 13, 2024, SpaceX conducted its fifth Starship flight test, which marked a significant achievement in the company's quest for reusability. This mission was notable for being the first time an orbital-class rocket was caught mid-air after launch. The Starship Ship 30 upper stage and Super Heavy Booster 12 lifted off from SpaceX's Starbase in Boca Chica, Texas.

After successfully delivering the Starship upper stage into a suborbital trajectory, the Super Heavy booster executed a controlled return to its launch site. For the first time, it performed a "catch" maneuver using two massive robotic arms known as "Mechazilla." As it approached the launch pad, the booster slowed down and aligned itself with these arms before they closed around it, effectively capturing it mid-air.

This mission not only demonstrated advanced recovery techniques but also showcased SpaceX's commitment to making its launch systems fully reusable. Following this test flight, NASA Administrator Bill Nelson praised SpaceX's achievement as a significant step forward in aerospace engineering

Kepler-452b

Kepler-452b, often referred to as "Earth's cousin," is a significant exoplanet discovered by NASA's Kepler mission in 2015. It is located approximately 1,400 light-years away in the constellation Cygnus and orbits a star that is similar to our Sun, known as Kepler-452. This star emits about 20% more light than our star, providing Kepler-452b with slightly more solar energy than Earth receives

Kepler-452b has a radius approximately 1.6 times larger than Earth's, placing it in the category of "super-Earths." If its composition is rocky, its mass could be around five times greater than Earth's, resulting in a gravitational force that would make a person weigh significantly more on its surface24. Its position in the habitable zone suggests that it could support liquid water, provided it has a suitable atmosphere. However, the exact nature of its atmosphere remains unknown; it could range from being rocky and Earth-like to having a thick gaseous envelope similar to Neptune15. Studies indicate that if Kepler-452b maintains conditions similar to those on Earth, it could remain in the habitable zone for another 3 billion years, allowing ample time for life to potentially develop5.
